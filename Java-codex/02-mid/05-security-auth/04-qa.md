# Q&A

Q: AuthN vs AuthZ?
A: AuthN verifies identity; AuthZ verifies permissions.

Q: What is the filter chain?
A: Ordered security filters that process each request.

Q: Why use BCrypt?
A: It is slow and adaptive, making brute-force harder.

Q: How is JWT validated?
A: Verify signature, expiration, and claims.

Q: What is CSRF?
A: A browser attack that exploits authenticated cookies.

Q: What is CORS?
A: A browser policy for cross-origin requests.

Q: Where should authorization live?
A: In config rules and method-level annotations.

Q: Why avoid storing secrets in the repo?
A: They can leak and compromise systems.
