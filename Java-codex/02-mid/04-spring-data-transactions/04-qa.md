# Q&A

Q: What does `@Transactional` do?
A: Starts or joins a transaction and commits or rolls back on failure.

Q: What is propagation?
A: How transactions behave when calling other transactional methods.

Q: What is isolation?
A: The visibility of concurrent changes between transactions.

Q: Optimistic vs pessimistic locking?
A: Optimistic checks versions; pessimistic locks rows in advance.

Q: What is a lost update?
A: One transaction overwrites another without noticing.

Q: Why avoid long transactions?
A: They hold locks and reduce concurrency.

Q: What is a dirty read?
A: Reading uncommitted data from another transaction.

Q: Where should transactions live?
A: At the service layer, around use cases.
